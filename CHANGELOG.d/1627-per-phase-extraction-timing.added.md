Per-phase extraction CPU-time breakdown — `pincher.index.extraction.per_phase` slog.Info line carrying `delete_syms_ms` (pre-extract DeleteSymbolsForFile) / `extract_ms` (safeExtractWithModule CPU) / `post_extract_ms` (everything else in the per-file goroutine: buffer mgmt, ReplacePendingEdges, ReplaceStructFields, ReplaceInterfaceMethods, SetFileHash, mid-batch flushBuffers, UpsertFile) — summed across all per-file goroutines. Validates #1627 hypothesis: on pincher-repo force-reindex, post-extract DB writes dominate **89%** of cumulative goroutine CPU-time while extractor CPU is only **0.04%** — DB-write contention through the single SQLite writer pool is the real bottleneck, not extractor speed. Note: numbers include lock-wait (goroutines blocked on the writer mutex accumulate wall-clock as their "time"), so they're inflated by overlap; the meaningful signal is the ratio, not the absolute.
